BakeXPress

Overview

Le Bread XPress is a food robotics company that provides high-tech solutions for the food service industry. At LBX I led the design for the go-to-market strategy of their hot food vending machine solution BakeXPress and was responsible for conducting competitive analysis, evaluating the tool, and developing new features such as a customer feedback system.

Roles

  • User Experience Design
  • User Experience Research

Timeline

February 2023 - May 2023

I. Concept

Challenge

Bake XPress is a hot food vending kiosk that prepares and delivers hot food to customers in seconds to people working long hours in need of fresh food. As the product entered its go-to-market phase, I was responsible for reviewing the existing UI, refining the customer journey, and developing features that filled gaps in the user flows.

II. Research

Competitive Analysis

I began my research by conducting competitive analysis to learn from current best practices and identify valuable gaps in the market. I worked closely with my team’s business analyst to find both direct and indirect competitors of LBX.


Competitor 1

The first competitor I investigated was an automated vending machine that grills, assembles, and delivers burgers in minutes. As another hot food vending machine it served as a direct competitor to LBX, making its features and business practices crucial to analyze.


The competitor’s start menu took the opportunity to emphasize its brand tone with a message reading “Hello Human”, highlighting the automated nature of the machine in a playful way to call attention to the marvel of a hot food vending machine and connect with users.


BakeXPress also takes advantage of its start screen to establish its brand tone with the message “Delicious Hot Food Awaits!” and accompanying image of a hot pizza with steam rising off it. The screen conveys the warmth and comfort of a hot meal and imparts its associations to the food inside the machine and the company’s brand.


Competitor 2

The second competitor I examined was a chain of smart vending machines that provide health and wellness products to those on the go. As an indirect competitor it was important to study to discover beneficial features or business practices that could be translated to BakeXPress


At the end of the checkout process for the competitor’s machine the user was given the option to leave feedback regarding their experience using the machine.


LBX’s BakeXPress machine did not have a way to gather customer feedback, which is crucial in understanding the user’s experience and discovering pain points that can be improved, so I proposed a feedback screen should be added to the user journey.


Following my research I met with key stakeholders at LBX to present my findings. They agreed a feedback screen would be beneficial to include. It was also brought into my attention that user-friendly error messages were to be developed.


III. Ideation

Design Solutions

Customer Feedback Screen

I designed the feedback screen to give users both quantitative and qualitative review options.


The first method was a zero to five star rating. This is a quick and straightforward way for the user to give feedback, and would provide a beneficial general idea of the user experience.


The second method is a qr code which links to a feedback forum. This outlet for longer-form feedback gives more in-depth insight into the user’s thoughts, and by implementing it via a QR code it is easy for users to access from their phones and allows it to be filled out later when a user may have more time to give a thoughtful response.


User-Friendly Error Messages

Internal stakeholders at LBX expressed concern about the current design of errors on the LBX BakeXpress machine, believing the bright color, and technical language would be confusing and concerning to customers.


To address these concerns I composed user friendly error messages explaining the issues in language more digestible to the everyday user.


I ensured my language maintained consistency with the established brand tone by utilizing friendly but not overly apologetic or humorous language. Additionally, I employed an image of a spilled cup in the error to add personality to the message in-line with the machine’s purpose as a food vending machine.


‘Out of order’ messages were positioned to appear on the start screen to alert users of issues before starting their journey to prevent frustration, and also allow the machine to continue advertising its products to encourage users to return later.


IV. Testing

Usability Testing

To determine the best position for the feedback screen along the user journey, I conducted a usability test between two different user flows. In the first flow, the user feedback screen appeared as a pop up on the screen that counts down until the user’s order is ready. I believed presenting the feedback options here may incentivize users to make use of the time waiting for their food to leave feedback. In the second flow the user feedback screen would appear after the countdown screen and the user received their order at the end of the user journey.


Based on users’ interactions and comments during testing, I observed that more users skipped the feedback screen in flow one when it was placed on the same screen as the countdown. One user reported that the count down timer felt too short for them to provide feedback before their order was ready, so they skipped the feedback screen.


Due to this feedback I positioned the screen after the countdown to prevent perceived interruptions to the order delivery process and to ensure reviews would be representative of the full user journey.


V. Outcome

Interactive Prototype

Bake XPress was presented at the NAMA Show 2023, a convention centered around client and consumer convenience experiences and received praise from many attendants.


An interactive prototype can be found on Figma.
